Preface
Handbook purpose and scope
+

This hand-book contains the official rules and regulations for the IFWL league. This guide is provided as a reference; to give the driver a concise and easy to use manual, laid out in a simple format, to easily reference IFWL rules and regulations.

This hand-book is meant to apply to all series/events governed by IFWL.net. General rules and regulations, as well as any specific rules and regulations pertaining to a specific series/event, are broken into sections and listed in the table of contents.

This guide is also intended to work in unison with any rules/regs posted on the Discord server. As with any prototype/work in progress, IFWL may change the rules and regulations, as necessary, at any point of time during any racing series that it hosts.

From all of us, thank-you for your participation and support.

Compliance
Driver compliance with IFWL rules & regulations
+

Drivers that complete the process of registering for entry into the IFWL and are assessed for placement within the IFWL, are agreeing to follow and obey the rules & regulations administered by the IFWL racing series staff and stewards.

The driver, by registering and being assessed by the IFWL, agrees to abide by and follow any and all final decisions rendered by the IFWL stewards and the IFWL staff.

Automation notice

IFWL operates automation within backend code — running between SimGrid / Discord / IFWL.net / IFWL PC App / IFWL Ticket System.

Offence tracking

The IFWL PC App tracks offences and stewards will follow guidelines — repeated breaches will flag behaviour and can result in removal/bans.

SimGrid ToS escalation

If driving is severe enough to breach SimGrid’s Terms of Service, IFWL will approach SimGrid and officially report behaviour such as intentional ramming / race obstruction / “red mist” incidents.

Final 3 exclusion

No new registrations/signups to existing events for Season 1 will be allowed in the final 3 races — keeping the field clean to committed drivers.

Always remember: be safe, follow the rules, obey the stewards, and most of all have fun.

Section I
General Rules and Regulations

1
Membership and Basic Series Entry Requirements
+
A

Drivers must remain in the official IFWL Discord server throughout the entirety of the active season. Leaving or unregistering the driver’s profile from the IFWL Discord server will result in removal of the driver from all active SimGrid events they are registered to, negatively impacting attendance and SimGrid rating.

B

IMPORTANT: The driver’s Discord profile name and number must match exactly to the information provided to IFWL.net and SimGrid. Any errors or deviation will result in driver disqualification. Driver number on SimGrid and IFWL.net/Discord must match.

C

A Patreon subscription is required for entry into any prize pools created by IFWL. IFWL is not “pay-to-win” and Patreon does not influence sign-up options or driver placement assessment. (At time of the document: payout system active for Pro-Am on PC only; console payout methods TBA; no payout system live for the test season.)

D

All required DLC packs must be owned as specified in the series season announcement on SimGrid before participating. No drop rounds — missing DLC or disliking a track and failing to attend results in point penalties.

E

Driver sign-up must be completed prior to 1.5 hours before race start to allow IFWL admin/staff time to prepare servers and minimize downtime.

  • Commentary/Broadcast: No

  • Start: 19:30 UK (BST/GMT)
  • Format: Practice 15m / Quali 15m / Race 60m
  • Pit: 1 mandatory — must change tyres + must take on fuel
  • Weather: Real-world weather disabled
  • Assists: Limited — pit speed limiter & lights on manual
  • Car changes: No changes (choice set for entire series)
  • Notes: Mixed entry field (amateurs and pros—relegated)
Console Open-Track Series
  • Start: 19:30 UK (BST/GMT)
  • Format: Practice 10m / Quali 20m / Race 45m
  • Pit: 1 mandatory — must change tyres
  • Weather: Real-world weather disabled
  • Assists: Limited — pit speed limiter & lights on manual
  • Car changes: No changes
  • Live stewards: Limited
  • Commentary/Broadcast: Yes (console stream)
Friday Night Finesse (GT4/GTC)
  • Start: 19:30 UK (BST/GMT)
  • Format: Beginners GT4 only / Pro-Am GTC only / 2× sprints varying lengths
  • Success ballast for P1; class and overall leaderboard; 2pts fastest lap
  • Finale: 70 min — 1 mandatory fuel stop
  • Weather: Variable announced week prior (time not fixed)
  • Car changes: No changes (choice set for entire series)
  • Live stewarding: Limited live stewarding
  • Commentary/Broadcast: Yes — full coverage
RaceRoom Open-Track Touring SRS (WTCR)
  • Start: 19:30 UK (BST/GMT)
  • Format: Custom practice / custom qualifying / two race set-up
  • Details provided per race
  • Car changes / settings / stewarding / commentary: found on IFWL.net website
  • Notes: Touring car regulations apply; all events hosted through IFWL.net (site has standings + points)
Event Format
The Big 4 Team Enduro — Official Event Rules & Format
+
Championship Overview

Multi-round team endurance championship featuring long-format races at iconic circuits. All events run with variable weather and are designed to test consistency, strategy, and teamwork.

Week 1 — Mandatory Testing

Date: 11th January

Track: Spa-Francorchamps (base game)

  • Practice: 15 minutes
  • Qualifying: 15 minutes
  • Race Simulation: 120 minutes

Server opens for practice 1 week prior.

Race Events (Weeks 2–5)

All race events follow:

  • Practice: 60 minutes
  • Qualifying: 15–20 minutes (track dependent)
  • Race: 180 minutes
  • Weather: Variable
  • 10-minute break between qualifying and race
  • No mandatory pit stops
Event Schedule
  • Week 2 (18 Jan): Spa — Base Game
  • Week 3 (25 Jan): Watkins Glen — American Track Pack
  • Week 4 (1 Feb): Bathurst — Intercontinental GT Pack
  • Week 5 (8 Feb): Nürburgring 24H — Nürburgring 24H Pack
Team & Driver Regulations
  • Each team must complete a minimum of one (1) driver swap per race.
  • The starting driver is not required to take part in qualifying.
  • Driver swaps may occur at any point during the race unless restricted by server limitations.
Race Day Operations
  • All races take place on Sunday.
  • Official race start time: 15:00 UTC.
  • Teams must be present and prepared for all sessions.
Weather & Conditions
  • Variable weather enabled for all sessions.
  • Teams responsible for adapting strategy to weather changes.
  • No restarts granted due to weather conditions.
Sporting Conduct
  • The standard IFWL sporting code applies at all times.
  • Unsafe driving, exploits, or unsporting behaviour may result in penalties or disqualification.
  • All decisions made by IFWL Race Control are final.

Racing Rules for All Drivers
+
A

Respect all staff, stewards, and drivers.

B

No dangerous behavior (ramming, brake-checking, dive-bombing, blocking, punting, etc.).

C

Overtaking: the overtaking driver is responsible for executing a safe passing move.

D

Defensive driving: one move allowed; must be performed prior to braking.

E

When in a spin hold the brakes; when rejoining, any unsafe re-entry will be penalized.

F

Driving in the wrong direction relative to the race leads to an instant disqualification.

G

All lapped drivers must yield under blue flags; lapped drivers may un-lap if faster but cannot defend.

H

All vehicles must comply with IFWL BoP (e.g., ACC uses Low Fuel Motorsport for BoP settings).

6
Stewards, Tickets, and Appeals
+
A

Incident ticket window: immediately after race completes until 24 hours after completion.

B

Appeals window: begins immediately after incident ticket window closes and lasts 24 hours.

C

Final decision: concluded and posted within 24 hours of the close of the appeals window.

D

Total “ticket → appeal → decision” process expected to conclude 72 hours after race completed.

E

Reporting drivers must save any and all replays/streams that provide evidence to the claim.

F

Decision timeframe may be extended due to ticket volume or other mitigating circumstances.

Section IV
Addendums and Erratta
+

The layout of previous sections (section #, area #, and line letter) is designed to make mid-season changes easier to notate and then officially add.

Example: a change to 1-5C would be a change to rule “C” in Section I, area 5.

Example entry format

An asterisk (*) would be placed on the appropriate line being changed, with a coinciding * on the addendum entry. Date placement is flexible.

*01/05/2026 – CHANGE TO 1-5C: (example text shown in the PDF)

Revision Notes (as listed)

  • 11-29-2025 — Admin revision notes supplied
  • 12-01-2025 — Designer completion of first half of admin revision notes
  • 12-07-2025 — Completed admin changes on revision 01
