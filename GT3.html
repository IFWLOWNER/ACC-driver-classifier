Teams
What every team must do
–
Discord & Driver Sign Up (required)
  • All teams are required to join the IFWL Discord.
  • All drivers must complete the IFWL Driver Sign Up (this unlocks Discord access).
  • Driver Sign Up can be completed by heading to IFWL.net and completing Driver Sign Up.
  • When complete, Discord names will format as: [ number ] - name
Race communications (required)
  • One person from every team is required to keep eyes on Race Control during the race.
  • This is the only place IFWL will officially call a Red Flag (server restart) whilst in-race.
  • If you miss a call because you are not watching Race Control, that is on the team.
Race control
Red flags, crashes, starts & restarts
+
Red flag triggers
  • Mass disconnects / server crash
  • Critical server instability impacting fairness/safety
  • Any issue where continuation of the session cannot be guaranteed
Red flag timeline (ACM GridMaker)

RED FLAG called

  • Admin posts RED FLAG in Race Control.
  • All drivers lift, return to pits safely, and await instructions.
  • No overtaking. No leaving unless instructed.

Cooldown / server OFFLINE (up to 30 minutes)

  • Server shuts down for up to 30 minutes.
  • Admin captures latest valid running order.
  • Admin generates the restart grid using ACM GridMaker.
  • All teams stay in Discord and watch Race Control.
  • Do not join the server early.

Server returns: PRACTICE (15–20 minutes)

  • Teams rejoin calmly, apply setups, and do any relevant driver swaps.
  • The restart-starting driver must join first (as confirmed by IFWL).

Server switches to RACE

  • Remaining race time applied (downtime removed where applicable).
  • ACM grid order applied.
  • Weather + time progression restored as closely as possible.

Restart

  • Short formation lap.
  • Single file, hold position.
  • No overtaking until Race Control calls GREEN.
Standard race start
  • 1 full formation lap
  • Aided rolling start
  • All cars remain in their green box until green flag
  • No overtaking before green flag
Lap 1 incident rule (start + restart)
  • Any team found predominantly at fault for causing a collision on Lap 1 will receive a Drive Through.
  • Once posted in live outcomes, the team has 5 laps to serve it.
  • For every lap late: an additional 10s Stop & Go applies.
  • IFWL may disqualify teams/drivers for driving below expected standards or poor Discord conduct.

Submit a Ticket Add Statement to Open Ticket
Incident reporting window
  • Opens immediately after the race concludes.
  • Closes 24 hours after the race ends.
  • Late reports will not be reviewed.
What to include
  • Lap number
  • Timestamp (or session time)
  • Cars/drivers involved
  • Short summary of what happened
  • Evidence: replay clip / stream VOD / screenshots (recommended)
  • Steward Code (if known — see codes)
Appeals
  • Appeals open immediately after the incident window closes.
  • Appeals remain open for 24 hours.
  • Final decisions typically posted within 24 hours after appeals close.
  • Expected end-to-end timeline: up to 72 hours after race finish.
